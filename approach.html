Approach

I focus on results that are reliable, explainable, and useful. The goal is not a “fancy model” — it’s a correct answer that supports real decisions.

1) Define the problem

Clarify the decision, success criteria, constraints, and what “good” looks like before analysis begins.

2) Audit the data

Assess completeness, consistency, definitions, and known edge cases. Identify data limitations early.

3) Clean and validate

Apply agreed cleaning rules and validation checks. Produce a clear quality summary so stakeholders can trust the results.

4) Analyse and model

Use appropriate statistical and analytical methods, prioritising robustness, interpretability, and validated performance.

5) Communicate and deliver

Deliver outputs that are clear and reproducible: findings, recommendations, assumptions, and limitations — with documentation suitable for both technical and non-technical audiences.
