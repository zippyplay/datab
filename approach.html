Approach

Practical, reproducible work — focused on reliable answers and decisions.

The goal is not a “fancy model”. The goal is a result you can trust — with assumptions made explicit, performance validated, and conclusions explained in plain language.

1) Define the decision

We start by clarifying the business question, success criteria, constraints, timelines, and what a useful output looks like (report, model, or code).

  • What decision needs to be made?
  • What does success look like?
  • What are the constraints (time, data, cost, risk)?

2) Audit the data

Before analysis, we assess completeness, consistency, definitions, and edge cases. This reduces surprises and avoids building on unreliable foundations.

  • Completeness, duplicates, missing values
  • Outliers and suspicious patterns
  • Metric definitions and data lineage

3) Clean and validate

Cleaning is done with agreed rules and documented checks. The aim is transparency: stakeholders should understand what changed and why.

  • Reproducible cleaning steps
  • Validation logic and sanity checks
  • Data quality summary and known limitations

4) Analyse and model

Methods are chosen to fit the data and the decision. We prioritise robustness, interpretability, and realistic performance, rather than black-box complexity.

  • Exploratory analysis to understand drivers
  • Statistical modelling / forecasting where appropriate
  • Cross-checks, validation, and error analysis

5) Communicate and deliver

Deliverables are structured so your team can use them immediately and confidently — with clear findings, recommendations, assumptions, and next steps.

  • Concise reporting for non-technical stakeholders
  • Reproducible analysis for technical teams
  • Documentation and optional walkthrough
